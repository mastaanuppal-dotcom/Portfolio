01 · About

Hands-on by default.

I learn by building. In my first two years of engineering I've designed and fabricated a motorized precision machine, assembled and validated PCBs with through-hole and surface-mount soldering, and worked two industry roles — one in naval architecture, one on a $4B+ transit megaproject.

What ties it together: I like the layer where electrons meet code. Reading a schematic, probing a board with an oscilloscope, then writing the firmware that brings it to life — that full loop is where I do my best work.

I'm now aiming that energy at the semiconductor and embedded systems industry — the companies designing the silicon that everything else runs on.
